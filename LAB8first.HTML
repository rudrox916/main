Historic Events

War is an armed conflict between the armed forces of states, or between governmental forces and armed groups that are organized under a certain command structure and have the capacity to sustain military operations, or between such organized groups. It is generally characterized by extreme violence, destruction, and mortality, using regular or irregular military forces. War often has profound political, economic, and social effects on the countries involved, and it frequently leads to the loss of life and infrastructure. For many nations, the impact of war can be felt for generations, influencing cultural memory and shaping future policies. Major wars, such as World War I, World War II, the Vietnam War, and others, have left lasting legacies on the global stage, altering the course of history. Understanding these events is essential for comprehending modern geopolitics and the efforts to prevent future conflicts.
